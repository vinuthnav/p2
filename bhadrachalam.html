Bhadrachalam

Bhadrachalam is a significant pilgrimage destination dedicated to Lord Rama, located on the banks of the Godavari River in Telangana. The Sri Sita Ramachandra Swamy Temple is believed to be over 350 years old and holds deep spiritual importance for devotees of Lord Rama. The temple was built by Kancherla Gopanna (Bhakta Ramadasu), a fervent devotee and Tahsildar under the Qutb Shahi rulers.

The temple is renowned for its Rama Navami celebrations, where the celestial wedding of Lord Rama and Sita is performed with grandeur. Thousands of devotees visit during this time to witness the divine event. Surrounded by lush greenery and river landscapes, Bhadrachalam also acts as a gateway to the tribal regions of Telangana.
