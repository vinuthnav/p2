Srisailam

Srisailam is home to the Mallikarjuna Swamy Temple, one of the 12 Jyotirlingas of Lord Shiva and also one of the 18 Shakti Peethas, making it a uniquely powerful site for both Shaivites and Shaktas. The temple sits atop the Nallamala Hills on the banks of the Krishna River in Andhra Pradesh. It is one of the most ancient temples, mentioned in scriptures and revered since the Satavahana period.

Pilgrims flock to Srisailam to worship Lord Mallikarjuna (Shiva) and Goddess Bhramaramba (Parvati). The temple architecture is a mix of Chalukyan and Vijayanagara styles, and the atmosphere is spiritually charged, especially during Maha Shivaratri. Dense forests and serene surroundings add to the temple's sanctity and appeal.
